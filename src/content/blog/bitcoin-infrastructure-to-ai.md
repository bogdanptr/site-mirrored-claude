Many sites originally built for bitcoin mining already have one of the hardest things to find: substantial power infrastructure already in place.

## Shared fundamentals

Both workloads care about cheap, reliable power and efficient cooling, which means existing mining sites often have a head start.

## What needs to change

Networking, rack layouts, and cooling systems typically need upgrades to support GPU workloads, but the electrical backbone can often be reused.

## A faster path to capacity

For operators looking to move quickly, retrofitting an existing power site can be significantly faster than starting from raw land.
