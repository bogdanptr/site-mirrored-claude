A data center's electrical distribution system is made up of several layers, each responsible for stepping down and routing power safely to IT equipment.

## Utility interconnect and substation

Power enters the site at high voltage and is stepped down at an on-site substation before distribution begins.

## Medium-voltage distribution

Medium-voltage switchgear and transformers route power to individual buildings or pods across the site.

## Low-voltage distribution and UPS

At the rack level, UPS systems and power distribution units (PDUs) provide clean, conditioned power with backup in case of an outage.
