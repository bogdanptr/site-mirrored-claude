A medium-voltage substation steps power down from utility voltage to a level that can be distributed safely across a site.

## Core components

Switchgear, transformers, and protection relays work together to step down voltage and protect downstream equipment from faults.

## Sizing considerations

Substations are typically sized with future expansion in mind, since adding capacity later can be far more disruptive.

## Maintenance access

Good substation design leaves room for maintenance access without requiring a full shutdown of the site.
